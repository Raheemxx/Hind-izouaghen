HIND IZOUAGHEN
Painter | Based in Agadir

About the Artist

Hind Izouaghen is a 31-year-old painter based in Agadir, Morocco. Coming from a family deeply rooted in the arts, creativity has always been part of her environment her father is a music teacher, her sister a recognized pianist, and her brother a guitarist and bassist. This artistic foundation shaped her sensitivity to expression, rhythm, and emotion from an early age. Entirely self-taught, Hind has reached an academic level in her work through discipline, observation, and continuous practice. Her journey outside traditional art schools gives her a unique position: technically refined, yet creatively and free from rigid frameworks.

Artistic Vision

Hind’s work is centered around three core pillars:
• Women empowerment
• Amazigh identity
• Facial expression and detail

She portrays women as strong, introspective figures, often emphasizing facial features to capture depth, resilience, and silent narratives. Her paintings celebrate Amazigh heritage, not as folklore, but as a living identity modern, evolving, and powerful.

Style & Influence

• Medium: Painting (primarily canvas)
• Style: Contemporary figurative with academic influence
• Key Focus: Facial detail, emotional realism, symbolic color use

Her work draws inspiration from classical Italian masters, particularly the precision and anatomical understanding associated with Leonardo da Vinci. This influence appears in her attention to proportion, subtle shading, and expressive faces, while her themes remain deeply personal and culturally grounded. She’s also familiar with the works of Cesar Santos, John Fenerov, and Casey Baugh.

Artistic Approach

Hind approaches each painting as a study of emotion and identity. She often begins with the face the most expressive element building the composition around it. Her process combines careful structure with intuitive decisions, allowing each piece to evolve naturally. The result is a balance between classical discipline and contemporary storytelling.

Artist Statement

“My work is a tribute to women, identity, and heritage. Through faces, I try to reveal what words cannot strength, memory, and belonging. I am deeply inspired by Amazigh culture and by the precision of classical art, especially the Italian masters. Each painting is a way of preserving identity while expressing the quiet power of women.”
